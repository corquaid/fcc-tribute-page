John W. Young

A Tribute to The Astronaut's Astronaut





John W. Young was an American astronaut and naval aviator. Regarded as a space pioneer, he flew to space on six occasions, walked on the Moon and flew four classes of spacecraft including the first crewed flights of both Gemini and the Space Shuttle.

Timeline of John Young's life:

  • 1930 - Born in San Francisco, California.

  • 1932 - Moved to Georgia and subsequently to Orlando, Florida during the Great Depression.

  • 1952 - Graduated from Georgia Tech with a Bachelor's degree in Aeronautical Engineering.

  • 1953 - Receives a commission in the US Navy serving in the Korean War.

  • 1956 - Marries Barbara White from Georgia with whom he has two children.

  • 1959 - As a naval aviator, Young is assigned to the Naval Air Test Center at Patuxent River, Maryland.

  • 1962 - Joins NASA as part of Astronaut Group 2 having set two world records for "time-to-climb" earlier in the year.

  • 1965 - First spaceflight as pilot of Gemini 3 with commander Gus Grissom.

  • 1966 - Second spaceflight as commander of Gemini 10 with pilot Michael Collins.

  • 1969 - Third spaceflight onboard Apollo 10 as Command Module Pilot, becoming the first person to fly solo on orbit around the Moon.

  • 1971 - Divorces from his first wife and remarries the same year to Susy Feldman.

  • 1972 - As commander of Apollo 16 becomes the 9th person to walk on the Moon upon landing in the Descartes Highlands.

  • 1974 - Becomes Chief of the Astronaut Office within NASA following Alan Shepherd's retirement.

  • 1981 - Flies his fifth spaceflight as commander of STS-1, the very first space shuttle flight aboard Columbia with Bob Crippen as pilot.

  • 1983 - Young's sixth and final spaceflight as commander of STS-9, again on Columbia.

  • 1986 - Young becomes openly critical of NASA management following the Challenger disaster, prompting his managerial move away from the Astronaut Office.

  • 2004 - Retires officially from NASA following a 42-year career.

  • 2012 - Publishes his long-awaited autobiography, entitled Forever Young.

  • 2018 - Dies at the age of 87 in Houston, Texas. He is buried in Arlington National Cemetery.
